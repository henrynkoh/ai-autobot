## Post body (HTML-friendly)

<h2>Why a structured plan matters</h2>

Evaluating a property for AFH conversion means more than liking the floor plan. You need to line up zoning, city and county permits, WABO, fire marshal, and inspections—and the rules differ by jurisdiction. Without a clear roadmap, it’s easy to waste time or miss steps.

<h2>Introducing the AFH Conversion Planner</h2>

The AFH Conversion Planner is a simple 4-step tool that turns one property (or MLS number) into a single, shareable plan snapshot.

<strong>Step 1 — Property or MLS</strong><br>
Enter the listing number and/or full address. That’s your starting point.

<strong>Step 2 — City & county</strong><br>
Permits and inspections are local. The plan is tailored to the jurisdiction you choose.

<strong>Step 3 — Goals</strong><br>
What do you want in the next 30 days? In 12 months? Licensing, build-out, sale, refinance—your answers shape what “done” looks like.

<strong>Step 4 — Your profile</strong><br>
Are you an AFH provider, an investor, or a real estate agent? How familiar are you with AFH codes? The tool adapts the level of detail and focus to you.

At the end you get a <strong>plan snapshot</strong>: one summary you can copy into a doc, send to a permit expeditor, or feed into an AI workflow. The app also shows how a “cluster” of agents (strategy, UX, systems, compliance) would use that snapshot—so when you’re ready to automate, the structure is already there.

<h2>Who it’s for</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>AFH providers</strong> — Operators (current or aspiring) who want a clear path from property to license.</li>
<li><strong>Investors</strong> — Anyone underwriting or exiting AFH deals who needs a realistic timeline and checklist.</li>
<li><strong>Real estate agents</strong> — Anyone working AFH listings or buyers and wants solid talking points and next steps.</li>
</ul>
