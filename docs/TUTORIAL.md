# Tutorial — Your First AFH Plan in 10 Minutes

This tutorial walks you through one complete run: from opening the app to using the plan snapshot.

---

## Before you start

- The app is running at **http://localhost:3000** (see [QUICKSTART](QUICKSTART.md)).
- Have ready: one property (MLS and/or address), its city and county, and a rough idea of your 30-day and 12-month goals.

---

## Part 1: Property (Step 1)

1. Open the app. You’ll see **Step 1 of 4** and **Basic property / MLS info**.
2. **MLS Number**  
   - If you have an MLS number, enter it (e.g. `22123456`).  
   - If not, leave it blank.
3. **Property address**  
   - Enter at least the address (e.g. `1234 Example Ave, Federal Way, WA`).  
   - You need either MLS or address to continue.
4. Click **Next step**.

**Example:**

- MLS: `22123456`  
- Address: `1234 Example Ave, Federal Way, WA`

---

## Part 2: City & County (Step 2)

1. **City** — Enter the city where the property is (e.g. `Federal Way`).
2. **County** — Enter the county (e.g. `King` or `King County`).  
   Both fields are required.
3. Click **Next step**.

**Example:**

- City: `Federal Way`  
- County: `King County`

---

## Part 3: Goals (Step 3)

1. **30-day focus**  
   - What would make the next month a success?  
   - Examples: “Validate AFH viability,” “Rough budget and timeline,” “Talk to city planner.”
2. **12-month outcome**  
   - Where do you want to be in a year?  
   - Examples: “Licensed AFH with 6 residents,” “Stabilized NOI,” “Sale to AFH operator.”
3. You can fill one or both. Click **Next step**.

**Example (provider):**

- 30-day: “Confirm zoning allows AFH, get ballpark renovation cost, identify architect.”  
- 12-month: “Licensed AFH, 5 residents, first year of operations complete.”

---

## Part 4: Profile (Step 4)

1. **Primary role**  
   - Choose **Provider**, **Investor**, or **Real estate agent**.  
   - This changes how the agent cluster panel describes its focus.
2. **AFH / code knowledge**  
   - Choose **New to AFH**, **Some experience**, or **Very experienced / expert**.  
   - This affects the level of detail and jargon in the (future) agent output.
3. **Communities, mentors, or pages** (optional)  
   - List AFH Facebook groups, DSHS, local meetups, YouTube channels, etc.  
   - Example: “Washington AFH Network FB group, DSHS AFH training.”
4. Click **Launch AFH agent cluster**.

---

## Part 5: After launch

1. **Agent Cluster Overview**  
   - Status changes to “Running plan.”  
   - Each track (CTO, UX, Dev, Compliance) shows what it would do and how it adapts to your role and knowledge.
2. **Draft plan snapshot**  
   - Scroll to the snapshot card.  
   - It contains: property (MLS, address, city, county), your role and knowledge, goals, and communities.
3. **Using the snapshot**  
   - Copy the text and paste into:  
     - A Google Doc or Notion for your team  
     - A GitHub issue or shared folder for agent workflows  
     - An email to a permit expeditor or consultant  

---

## Part 6: Try different roles

1. Use **Back** to return to Step 4.
2. Change **Primary role** to **Investor** or **Real estate agent**.
3. Click **Launch AFH agent cluster** again.
4. Compare the right panel: the “focus” and “bias” lines for each track change with your role (e.g. returns and exit for Investor, listing language for Agent).
